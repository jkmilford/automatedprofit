01The gap nobody's talking about

Most marketers already agree that AI search matters. Far fewer have actually checked whether their own business shows up in it. That's the gap — and referral traffic arriving from AI answer engines has been climbing fast enough that "wait and see" is quietly turning into "get left behind."

Here's the uncomfortable part: that gap is either really good news or really bad news, depending on which side of it you're standing on. Right now, you don't know which side you're on. Almost nobody does.

02Why this isn't just "SEO with a new name"

Traditional SEO competes for a spot on a results list — the searcher does the work of choosing. AI answer engines skip the list. They read across the web, decide what's true and relevant, and hand the user one synthesized recommendation. There's no page two to hide on.

Thin, template-y websites get skipped. Vague "About Us" pages get skipped. Scattered, inconsistent information across the web gets skipped — or worse, described inaccurately.

That last part is the real risk. It's increasingly common for an AI model to confidently say something wrong about a real business — old hours, a wrong price, a service that isn't offered anymore. If a customer asks "is this place still open?" and gets a bad answer, you lose them before you ever know they existed.
